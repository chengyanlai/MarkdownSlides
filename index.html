Modeling: Effective single band model

  • Fermi Hubbard model in 1D (w/o core hole)

    $\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}=-J\sum_{\langle ij \rangle,\sigma}(d^\dagger_{i\sigma}d_{j\sigma}+h.c.)+U\sum_in_{i\uparrow}n_{i\downarrow}$

  • X-ray photon excites one electron from core level to valance band, and create a core hole which has an effective attractive potential.
  • For cuprates, $J\sim300-500$meV, $U\sim8J$ and $V_{pd}\sim1.3U$.

XAS: Formula

Effective one band model in one-dimension

$\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}\vert GS\rangle=E_0\vert GS\rangle\quad$ obtained by DMRG (MPS) with fixed filling $\bar{n}_f=\frac{N_f}{L}$

$\vert\Psi(t=0)\rangle=d^\dagger_{m\sigma}\vert GS\rangle\quad$ can be done in MPS formula

$\mathcal{H}_m(t\geq0)=\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}$$-V_{\text{ch}}\sum_\sigma n_{m\sigma}$ quench dynamics!

XAS from real time dynamics

\begin{eqnarray} I_{\text{XAS}}(\omega)&\propto&\sum_\sigma\sum_f \vert\langle f \vert d_{m\sigma}^\dagger \vert 0 \rangle\vert^2 \delta(E_f-E_0-\hbar\omega)\\ &=& \frac{1}{\pi}\sum_\sigma\text{Im}\left[ -i\int_0^\infty dt e^{i(\hbar\omega+E_0)t}e^{-\color{red}\Gamma t} \color{green}{\langle GS \vert d_{m\sigma} }\color{blue}{e^{-i\mathcal{H}_mt} d_{m\sigma}^\dagger \vert GS \rangle} \right]. \end{eqnarray} Non-equilibrium quench dynamics can be solved by TEBD (tDMRG)!

A. E. Feiguin and S. White, Phys. Rev. B 72, 020404 (2005); C.-Y. Lai and C.-C. Chien, Phys. Rev. A 96, 033628 (2017)

XAS: Equilibrium

$\bar{n}_f\!=\!1/3$ with $U\!=\!0$.

  • $V_\text{ch}=0$: The absorption part of DOS.
  • $V_\text{ch}>0$: Splitting due to core hole effect.
    • Single particle $\omega_s\sim -V_\text{ch}$.
    • Doubly occupied $\omega_d\sim U-2V_\text{ch}$.
    • Empty $\omega_e\sim0$.

The energy scale is corresponding to the bound state energy at the core hole site.

XAS: Different fillings (oxidation states)

$U\!=\!2J$ and $V_{ch}\!=\!12J$.

  • $\omega_d$ is robust as filling changes.
  • $\omega_s$ shows dependence on the different oxidation states.
  • The weight of each peak also changes.

    $\mathcal{W}_i=\int^{\omega_i+\delta\omega}_{\omega_i-\delta\omega}\mathcal{I}_\text{XAS}(\omega)d\omega$

    The weight shifts from $\omega_s$ to $\omega_d$ as the filling approaches 1.

  • Mott insulator: $\mathcal{W}_d>\mathcal{W}_s$
  • Metallic state:
    Dilute limit: $\mathcal{W}_s>\mathcal{W}_d$
    Dependence of $\bar{n}_f$, $U$, and $V_\text{ch}$.

$\bar{n}_f\!=\!1$ v.s. $\bar{n}_f\!=\!1/3$.

Correlation effects from XAS: Frequency shifts

$\bar{n}_f\!=\!1$ with $V_\text{ch}\!=\!12J$.

Correlation effects play an important role:

  • $\omega_d\!=\!U\!-\!2V_\text{ch}$ remains unchanged!
  • $\omega_s\!=\!-V_\text{ch}$ shifts! $\Rightarrow$ $\omega_s^\prime\!=\!U\!-\!V_\text{ch}$
    due to the ground state is a Mott insulator!

Correlation effects from XAS: Frequency shifts

  • Metallic state: $\Delta\omega\!=\!\omega_s-\omega_d\approx V_{ch}-U$
  • Mott insulator: $\Delta\omega\!=\!\omega_s-\omega_d\approx V_{ch}$

The XAS can capture the strong correlated effects!

Quantum Impurity Models of Kondo Physics

Single impurity interact with itinerant free fermions and bosonic bath.

  • Fermion bath: Kondo coherence.
  • Bosonic bath: Spin fluctuation.

Characterization of the bath is important

  • Zero DOS near low energy.
  • Finite DOS near low eneryg.
  • Finite but vanish at zero energy: critical?

Model and Bath Discretization

Bose-Fermi-Anderson impurity model

$H_{f}=-J\sum_{\sigma,i}(c^\dagger_{i\sigma}c_{i+1\sigma}+h.c.)-V_f\sum_\sigma(d^\dagger_{\sigma}c_{1\sigma}+h.c.)$

$+U_f(n_{d\uparrow}-\frac12)(n_{d\downarrow}-\frac12)$

$H_{b}=\sum_q\omega_q\phi^\dagger_{q}\phi_{q}$

$H_{int}={g}S_d^z\sum_q(\phi^\dagger_{-q}+\phi_{q})$, where $S_d^z=\frac12(n_{d\uparrow}-n_{d\downarrow})$.

Bosonic Bath:

$B(\omega)=\begin{cases} B_0\omega_0(\frac{\omega}{\omega_0})^s\quad 0<\omega<\omega_0, \\ 0\quad\text{otherwise}. \end{cases}$

Discretization
  • Logarithmic space: $\omega_0\Lambda^{-m}$.
  • Standard Lanczos technique to 1D chain.

Competing orders in equilibrium

$L\!=\!18(b)\!+\!54(f)$, $s=0.8$, $\omega_0\!=\!3J$

Small $B_0$: Kondo coherence

Large $B_0$: Localized

Curie constant $\vert S_d^z\vert$ becomes finite in large $B_0$.

$X_{-1} = \langle b_{-1} + b^\dagger_{-1} \rangle$: effective magnetic field to the impurity.

Post-pulse Signatures

$L\!=\!10(b)+30(f)$ with $A_0\!=\!1.2$, $\Omega\!=\!4J$ and $\tau\!=\!0.5t_0$

  • Stronger oscillation from Kondo coherent states and the period depends on both $U_f$ and $V_f$.
  • From the localized side, both correlations recover back to equilibrium value quickly.

Post-pulse Signatures (continued)

  • The period of oscillation doesn't strongly depend on pump pulse frequency (certain range), intensity, the bosonic bath ($s$) and the coupling $B_0$.
  • As the system moves toward localized side (large $B_0$), the amplitude becomes smaller.
