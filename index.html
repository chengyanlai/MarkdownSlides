Single band model with single X-ray photon event

  • Fermi Hubbard model in 1D (w/o core hole)

    $\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}=-J\sum_{\langle ij \rangle,\sigma}(c^\dagger_{i\sigma}c_{j\sigma}+h.c.)+U\sum_in_{i\uparrow}n_{i\downarrow}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}\vert GS\rangle=E_0\vert GS\rangle$

  • X-ray photon excites one electron from valance band to conduction band, and create a core hole which has effective attractive potential.

    $\vert\Psi(t=0)\rangle=c^\dagger_{ch}\vert GS\rangle;\quad\quad\mathcal{H}(t\geq0)=\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}$$-V_{\text{ch}}n_{ch}$.

How to calculate XAS?

Effective one band model in 1D

$\mathcal{H}_{\text{FHM}}\vert GS\rangle=E_0\vert GS\rangle\quad$ obtained by DMRG (MPS) with fixed filling $\bar{n}_f=\frac{N_\uparrow}{L}$

$\vert\Psi(t=0)\rangle=c^\dagger_{ch}\vert GS\rangle\quad$ can be done in MPS formula

$\mathcal{H}(t)=-J\sum_{\langle ij \rangle,\sigma}(c^\dagger_{i\sigma}c_{j\sigma}+h.c.)+U\sum_in_{i\uparrow}n_{i\downarrow}$$-V_{\text{ch}}n_{ch}$

XAS spectrum

\begin{eqnarray} I_{\text{XAS}}(\omega)&\propto&\sum_\sigma\sum_f \vert\langle f \vert c_{ch,\sigma}^\dagger \vert 0 \rangle\vert^2 \delta(E_f-E_0-\hbar\omega)\\ &=& \frac{1}{\pi}\sum_\sigma\text{Im}\left[ -i\int_0^\infty dt e^{i(\hbar\omega+E_0)t}e^{-\color{red}\Gamma t} \color{green}{\langle GS \vert c_{ch,\sigma} }\color{blue}{e^{-i\mathcal{H}t} c_{ch,\sigma}^\dagger \vert GS \rangle} \right]. \end{eqnarray} Non-equilibrium quench dynamics can be solved by TEBD (tDMRG)!

Quantum Impurity Models of Kondo Physics

Single impurity interact with itinerant free fermions and bosonic bath.

  • Fermion bath and impurity: Kondo coherence.
  • Bosonic bath and impurity: Spin fluctuation.

Consequences: Absence or presence of Kondo coherence or Critical behavior.

Model and Bath Discretization

Bose-Fermi-Anderson impurity model

$H_{f}=-J\sum_\sigma\sum_{i=1}^{L-1}(c^\dagger_{i\sigma}c_{i+1\sigma}+h.c.)-V_f\sum_\sigma(d^\dagger_{\sigma}c_{1\sigma}+h.c.)+U_f(n_{d\uparrow}-\frac12)(n_{d\downarrow}-\frac12)$

$H_{b}=\sum_q\omega_q\phi^\dagger_{q}\phi_{q}$

$H_{int}={g}S_z\sum_q(\phi^\dagger_{-q}+\phi_{q})$, where $S_z=\frac12(n_{d\uparrow}-n_{d\downarrow})$.

Bosonic Bath:

$B(\omega)=\begin{cases} B_0\omega_0(\frac{\omega}{\omega_0})^s\quad 0<\omega<\omega_0, \\ 0\quad\text{otherwise}. \end{cases}$

Discretization
  • Logarithmic space: $\omega_0\Lambda^{-m}$.
  • Standard Lanczos technique to 1D chain.

Signatures in Equilibrium

$L_b\!=\!18$ and $L_f\!=\!54$; Kondo energy $\propto V^2_f/U_f$.

Larger $B_0$ $\Rightarrow$ stronger coupling to bosonic degrees of freedom.

$X_{-1} = \langle b_{-1} + b^\dagger_{-1} \rangle$: oscillation of the bosonic mode next to the impurity.

Pump Pulse Effect

Time-dependent Peierls phase on hopping of electrons

$H_{f}(t)=-J\color{red}{e^{iA(t)}}\sum_\sigma\sum_{i=1}^{L-1}(c^\dagger_{i\sigma}c_{i+1\sigma}+h.c.)-V_f\color{red}{e^{iA(t)}}\sum_\sigma(d^\dagger_{\sigma}c_{1\sigma}+h.c.)\\\qquad\quad+U_f(n_{d\uparrow}-\frac12)(n_{d\downarrow}-\frac12)$
$H_b$ and $H_{int}$ unchanged.
The Pieles phase is a semiclassical form of the pulse field:
$A(t)=A_0e^{-(t-t_d)^2/2\tau^2}\cos\Omega (t-t_d)$.

Fidelity of the wave function

$\Omega\!=\!4J$ and $\tau\!=\!0.5$

On the localized side, the system is more robust against the pump pulse.

Post-pulse Signatures

  • Stronger oscillation from Kondo coherent states and the period depends on both $U_f$ and $V_f$.
  • From the localized side, both correlation recover back to equilibrium value quickly.

Post-pulse Signatures (continued)

  • The period of oscillation doesn't strongly depend on pump pulse frequency (certain range), intensity, the bosonic bath ($s$) and the coupling $B_0$.
  • As the system moves toward localized side (large $B_0$), the amplitude becomes smaller.

Conclusion and outlook

  • (Far-from-)Equilibrium X-ray absorption spectrum (XAS) in 1D strongly correlated systems can be studied by MPS in time domain.
  • Core hole effect are important and reveal in XAS.
  • Equilibrium XAS reveals the one particle excitation and the existence of charge gap at the half-filling.
  • Rich feature from TR-XAS and different influence are studied.
